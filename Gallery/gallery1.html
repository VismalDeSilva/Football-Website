FIFA WORLD CUP CHAMPIONS
2002-2022



Argentina-2022

Argentina won the FIFA World Cup 2022. Argentina defeated two-time champions France 4-2 on penalties in FIFA World Cup 2022 final tournament for the first time after 36 years and the third time overall.

Lionel Messi scored a devastating goal in the 108' minute to take the lead, but Kylian Mbappé equalized again to make the game 3-3 and take the game into penalty kicks. Messi became the first player in history to score in the Group Stages, Round of 16, Quarter-Final, Semi-Final, and Final in the same tournament.

France-2018

France were crowned champions for the second time in history and for the first since they were hosts in 1998 after defeating Croatia 4-2 in what will go down as one of the most thrilling World Cup finals ever.

Nineteen-year-old Kylian Mbappe became the second teenager in history to score in a World Cup Final, following in the footsteps of Pele who did so as a 17-year-old in 1958. Goals and performances from midfielder Paul Pogba and forward Antoine Griezmann also proved vital in helping Les Bleus become world champions once more.

Germany-2014

2014 was a special FIFA World CupTM, hosted by a nation that has come to represent the best of football. It was an extraordinary spectacle, with packed stadiums and passionate crowds treated to thrills, upsets and a record number of goals.

Germany reclaimed the Trophy they had won three times before and became the first European team to triumph in the Americas. Joachim Low's side were worthy and impressive winners, although for the third World Cup in succession it required extra time to decide the Final, with Mario Gotze assuming the role that Andres Iniesta had occupied in 2010.

Spain-2010

The 2010 FIFA World Cup was won by the Spanish national football team, who defeated the Netherlands 1-0 in the final. The tournament was held in South Africa from June 11 to July 11, 2010, and featured 32 teams from around the world.

Spain had a difficult path to the final, having lost their opening match to Switzerland, but they went on to win their remaining group stage matches and progressed to the knockout rounds. In the knockout stages, they defeated Portugal, Paraguay, Germany, and finally the Netherlands to lift the World Cup trophy for the first time in their history.

Italy-2006

The 2006 FIFA World Cup was won by the Italian national football team, who defeated France 5-3 on penalties in the final. The tournament was held in Germany from June 9 to July 9, 2006, and featured 32 teams from around the world.

The final between Italy and France was a dramatic match that ended in a 1-1 draw after extra time, with goals from Marco Materazzi for Italy and Zinedine Zidane for France. The match then went to a penalty shootout, where Italy emerged victorious thanks to successful spot kicks from Fabio Grosso, Marco Materazzi, Andrea Pirlo, Daniele De Rossi, and Fabio Grosso, and saves from goalkeeper Gianluigi Buffon.

Brazil-2002

The 2002 FIFA World Cup was won by the Brazilian national football team, who defeated Germany 2-0 in the final. The tournament was held in South Korea and Japan from May 31 to June 30, 2002, and featured 32 teams from around the world.

The final between Brazil and Germany was a closely contested match, with both teams creating chances but unable to score until Ronaldo scored twice in the second half to secure a 2-0 victory for Brazil. Ronaldo's goals in the final took his total tally for the tournament to 8 goals, making him the top scorer and earning him the Golden Boot award.
